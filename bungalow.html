Bungalow

Indian Bungalow : An Indian bungalow, today, typically refers to any standalone, single-family home, contrasting sharply with the common apartment living in urban India. Traditionally, the architectural style is characterized by a single or one-and-a-half-story structure, a low-pitched roof, and most notably, an expansive front porch or veranda. These design elements were originally a thoughtful response to the tropical climate, allowing for cross-ventilation, protection from direct sun and heavy monsoons, and a seamless blend of indoor and outdoor living. While the term's meaning has broadened to often signify status and a large plot of land, the core features of a detached house with a strong connection to its surrounding garden remain.
